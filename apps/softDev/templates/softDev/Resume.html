Susan Miller Dorsey High School
Biology Teacher (April 2017 - June 2019)

As a teacher, I played many roles at once. My main duty was to prepare lessons in alignment with the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS); but some of the other roles I filled to help my students included:

  • A counselor by looking through my students' transcripts and explaining what they needed to do to stay on track, or to catch up.
  • A therapist by always making myself available to them to talk through their problems, and offer them the best advice that benefitted them.
  • An interpretor. We had a new student check in to our school from Belarus, and I was the only Russian-speaking person on campus, so I helped enroll him.
  • An older brother/mentor by generally being there for them by telling them what they needed to hear, even if it wasn't what they wanted to hear. I would even have conferences with their other teachers with them present to come up with plans to help them succeed if they were struggling.

My driving belief was that I was there to do what was best for my students. In education, they are the clients, and I was always willing to go above and beyond for them.

Day to day, I was very flexible, and easily adapted to the many variables that each day could bring, all while maintaining a positive demeanor, and professionalism at all times. As a teacher, I experienced a lot of professional and personal growth by learning from my students, as they were learning from me.
